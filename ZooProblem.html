Zoo Animals

A Comprehensive Guide to taking care of Zoo Animals

Bears

Meet our large variety of black and brown bears, including our oldest residents:

  • Ollie
  • Mona

Do bears really eat honey? Contrary to what a popular icon would lead you to believe; bears diets mostly consist of fat and meat. Ollie and Mona's diets are salmon based with some frozen blackberries mixed in for an extra treat during the hot summer months. Grizzly and black bears eat mostly vegetation, insects, berries, and meat such as salmon, deer, and young mooses. Click here to find out more!


Giraffes

Our Resident Giraffes:

  • Frankie
  • Coconut

Did you know that a giraffe's neck is not long enough to reach the ground? In order to drink water, a giraffe will have to bend their knees and lower their necks to reach the surface of the water. Both Coconut and Frankie's diet consists of a mix of carrots, apples, seeds, and leaves placed at the top of artificial trees closely resemble their feeding habbits. Learn more interesting facts here!


Lions

Catch up with our lions:

  • Mella
  • Karl

African lions are the most social of all big cats, they live in a "pride" of up to 15 lions per group. Female lions are responsible for hunting while males defend the pride's territories. During the hot summer months, we take care of Mella and Karl by feeding them a frozen bloodcicle made of horse blood to help these big cats cool down! Learn more interesting facts about lions here.


Monkeys

Stop monkeyin' around and meet our monkeys!

  • Cookie
  • Earl
  • Banana Pudding

At this zoo, our most popular residents deserve high quality fruits, nuts, seeds, insects, and leaves. We provide trees and toys to further enrich our resident monkeys lives. Careful of Cookie though! She loves to play and will often play tricks with the keeper in charge of feeding for the day. Earl and Cookie favor fruits and nuts over seeds, but Banana Pudding, as his name implies, loves fruits! To learn more facts about monkey's click here.


Alligators

Meet the Alligators:

  • Wren
  • Aspen
  • Mika

Alligators are members of the crocodilian family. A Cold-blooded hunter, alligators have evolutionally undergone very little change over 84 million years ago. Unlike crocodiles, alligators cannot swim in saltwater habitats because they lack the glands to excrete salt from their bodies. An alligators diets consists of a combo of rodents, poultry, fish, and meat-based diets.
Wren especially loves a good scrub at the end of the week, so give him a good brush over when cleaning out their environment!
